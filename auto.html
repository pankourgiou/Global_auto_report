🌏

Asia dominates production. Asia (inc. Oceania) produced 54.9 million vehicles in 2024. China alone: 31.3M — up 4% YoY and 5.5M higher than 2019. India grew 3% to 6M units. Meanwhile Europe produced only 17.2M — down from 21.2M in 2019.

🛡️

Tariff shock of 2025. After Trump's 25% tariff on global cars announced April 2025, production and shipment patterns became chaotic. Low-priced models may disappear from key markets. The global automotive trade is being fundamentally restructured.

📈

Latin America surged. Brazil hit 2.63M vehicle sales — a 14.1% YoY jump. South America's truck production surged 37.1%. Eastern Europe and Russia also saw strong rebounds, with Russia producing 980,000 units — up 35% from 2023.

⚡

BYD's dominance is structural. BYD controls its entire supply chain — battery chemistry, chips, motors, final assembly. Its cheapest model (Seagull) costs under $10,000. It employs 968,900 people — the world's largest manufacturing employer. In 2025, BYD is expected to outsell Tesla globally by 45%.

🇺🇸

USA lags behind. Only 10% of US car sales in 2024 were EVs — half the global average. Tesla's US share fell from 75% in 2022 to below 50% in 2024. The $7,500 Clean Vehicle Tax Credit helped, but political headwinds and range anxiety remain barriers.

🌍

Global South leapfrogging. In 2024: Brazil EV sales doubled, Indonesia tripled, Malaysia doubled, Singapore jumped 164%. Over 80% of car imports to Nepal and Sri Lanka were EVs in 2024. Affordable Chinese EVs are driving unprecedented adoption in emerging markets.

🤖

Software is eating cars. The biggest changes are automation and electrification. Automotive semiconductors are the new bottleneck. Software-defined vehicles (SDVs) represent a fundamental shift — cars becoming rolling computers. The 2021–22 chip shortage cost the industry an estimated $210B in lost production.

🌍

China's export surge. Chinese manufacturers produced 5.5M more vehicles in 2024 than 2019. Their exports are flooding Europe, Southeast Asia, and Latin America with affordable EVs. EU imposed tariffs up to 45% on Chinese EVs in 2024. Trade wars are reshaping the global supply chain.

🔋

Battery costs collapsed. Lithium-ion battery pack prices fell from $1,200/kWh in 2010 to under $100/kWh in 2024 — a 92% reduction. At $80/kWh (expected by 2026) EVs reach price parity with ICE vehicles in most markets without subsidies. The tipping point is near.

Section 08 — Historical Timeline

120 Years of Automotive History

From Ford's Model T to Tesla's Cybertruck — the automotive industry's most defining moments, crashes, revivals, and revolutions.

Global Vehicle Sales — Long-Term View

Millions of units annually — major market events shown

🚗
1908

Ford Model T — Mass Motorisation Begins

Henry Ford introduces the Model T at $825 ($26,000 today). Assembly line production by 1913 cut price to $550. By 1927: 15 million sold. The car went from luxury to necessity in 20 years.

📉
2008–2009

Financial Crisis — GM's $91B Bankruptcy

Global car sales collapsed. General Motors declared the largest industrial bankruptcy in US history at $91B. US government bailout of $49.5B. Chrysler also went bankrupt. Detroit nearly died.

⚡
2012

Tesla Model S — Premium EVs Proven Viable

Tesla Model S wins Motor Trend Car of the Year. Proves EVs can compete on performance, range, and desirability. Opens the era of premium electrification. Tesla stock: $6 (split-adjusted). Today: $300+.

🦠
2020–2022

COVID + Chip Shortage — $210B Lost

COVID shut plants globally. Then semiconductor shortage hit harder. Industry lost an estimated $210B in production. Average new car prices surged 30%+ in the US. Waiting lists stretched 12+ months.

🇨🇳
2023–2024

China Overtakes Germany as World's Top Car Exporter

China became the world's #1 car exporter in 2023, surpassing Germany. Exported 4.9M vehicles. BYD crossed 3M annual sales. The global automotive power balance shifted irrevocably eastward.

🤖
2025–2030

The Autonomous & Software-Defined Era

Waymo, Cruise, Tesla FSD, BYD's DiPilot and others scale robotaxis. The EU's 2035 ICE ban forces legacy automakers to bet everything on EVs. Battery prices reach parity. The 120-year ICE era ends.
